Mango Sandwich

Made with just about 2-3 major ingredients, the mango sandwich is another fun way to include mango in your diet. Chill in the fridge before eating or use chilled ingredients for the best experience!

Ingredients

  • 8-10 Mango Slices (Thin)
  • 1 Bowl Cream/Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp Powdered Sugar
  • 1 tbsp Mango puree
  • 1/2 tsp Cardamom powder

Method

  1. In a bowl, mix together cream/Greek yogurt, cardamom powder, sugar, and mango puree (finely smashed mango pieces also work). Let it chill in the fridge.
  2. Take bread slices and cut the brown edges.
  3. Spread the chilled cream mix on the bread slices, place 4-5 mango slices on each bread, and sandwich with another slice of bread.
  4. Keep in the fridge for 5 mins more if you want or enjoy it as is!

Paneer Bhurji Sandwich

Paneer bhurji sandwich is a simple, easy and tasty sandwich that can be made for breakfast, evening snacks or for a light lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 4 Slices Bread
  • 2 tsp Butter
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1 tsp Grated Garlic
  • Salt
  • A pinch of Turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp Red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/4 tsp Dry Mango powder
  • 1 medium Onion
  • 1/3 cup Paneer, crumbled

Method

  1. In a kadhai, heat oil and saute garlic and cumin seeds. Remove it from heat.
  2. Add paneer, red chilli powder, masala powder, turmeric, salt, coriander leaves, amchur powder and mix it well.
  3. Spread butter on the bread, place onion rings, tomatoes and paneer bhurji. Close the sandwich, and grill it.
  4. Serve and enjoy!

Keema Naan

Keema Naan: This is a delicious naan recipe, stuffing is prepare with mince mutton & spices. Spicy stuffing makes this keema naan complete meal, you can pair this with raita or your favorite gravy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Maida
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 cups curd
  • 5-6 tbsp neutral oil
  • Water as required
  • 1 cups minced mutton
  • 1/2 cups onions ,chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder

Method

  1. In a bowl add maida, salt, sugar, some oil, curd and water to knead a soft dough. Pat with some oil and let this rest for around 2 hours.
  2. In a pan heat some oil; add ginger garlic paste, minced meat and onions.
  3. Cook for some time and then add the powdered spices like cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, haldi, red chilli powder and salt according to taste.
  4. Once the keema is almost cooked, add coriander leaves and take off the heat
  5. Get the dough and section into 4. Make thick medium sized rotis and add the stuffing in them.

Masala Cheese Toast

As you can tell by the name, this toast recipe is filled with a host of tangy and spicy masala and cheese that gives a burst of flavour in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 Chopped onion, capsicum and carrot
  • 1/2 tsp Red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp Pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Coriander Powder
  • Salt as per taste
  • 2 Breads
  • Cheese

Method

  1. To make this dish first take chopped onion, capsicum and carrot.
  2. Throw in masalas like red chilli, pepper, coriander powder and salt as per taste.
  3. Cook till the veggies turn soft. Once done, take a piece of bread and lightly toast it from one side.
  4. Now add the prepared masala on the toasted side (allowing the other side to also cook.)
  5. Then add cheese from top and let it melt.
